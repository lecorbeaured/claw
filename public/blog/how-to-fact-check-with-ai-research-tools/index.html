Step-by-Step Process for AI-Powered Fact Checking

Effective fact checking with AI research tools follows a systematic approach. CLAW users can verify claims in three structured phases that mirror professional journalism standards.

Phase 1: Isolate the Specific Claim

Begin by identifying the exact assertion requiring verification. Vague queries produce vague results. Instead of asking "Is climate change real?", formulate specific queries like "What was the global average temperature increase in 2025 according to NASA?" CLAW's Research mode performs best with precise, measurable claims.

Phase 2: Request Multi-Source Verification

Ask CLAW to find the claim from multiple independent sources. A reliable fact appears consistently across authoritative outlets. CLAW's Research mode can simultaneously search news archives, academic databases, government publications, and primary sources—a process that would take humans 45-60 minutes compressed into approximately 15 seconds.

Phase 3: Trace to Primary Sources

Secondary reporting often introduces errors. Request that CLAW locate the original study, statement, or dataset behind any claim. The platform's Research mode excels at finding primary documents and providing direct links for independent verification.
