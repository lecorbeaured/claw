Best Practices for AI-Assisted Email Writing

Effective AI email writing requires more than typing "write me an email." The quality of output directly correlates with the specificity of input. Following these practices maximizes the value of tools like CLAW.

1. Provide Context About the Relationship

Tell CLAW whether you're writing to a new prospect, long-term client, supervisor, or colleague. Include relevant history: "This is our third email exchange" or "We met at a conference last month." Context shapes tone and formality levels appropriately.

2. Specify the Desired Outcome

Every email should have a clear goal. State it explicitly: "I want them to schedule a call" or "I need them to approve the budget by Friday." CLAW structures the email to drive toward that specific action.

3. Indicate Tone Preferences

Words like "formal," "friendly," "apologetic," "confident," or "urgent" dramatically alter the output. A request to "write a friendly but professional follow-up" produces different results than "write a firm reminder about the overdue payment."

4. Review Before Sending

AI generates excellent drafts, but human review remains essential. Check that names, dates, and specific details are accurate. Add personal touches that reference shared experiences or inside knowledge the AI couldn't possess.
